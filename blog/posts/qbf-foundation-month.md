## 1. How Quantum Computing Is Different from Classical Computing


Classical computers work with **bits**:

* `0` or `1`

Quantum computers work with **qubits**:

* `0`
* `1`
* **both at the same time**

This is called **superposition**.

Instead of saying:

> “The computer checks one option at a time.”

Quantum says:

> “Let’s explore many possibilities at once.”

This is not “faster computing”.
It is a **different kind of computing**.



## 2. Understanding the Language of Quantum

Before this badge, these words felt mystical. Now they feel logical:

| Term            | Meaning                                                            |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Qubit           | The quantum version of a bit                                       |
| Superposition   | A qubit can exist in multiple states simultaneously                |
| Entanglement    | Qubits can be linked so the state of one instantly affects another |
| Quantum Gates   | Operations that change the state of qubits                         |
| Quantum Circuit | A sequence of quantum gates applied to qubits                      |

I now know that quantum programming is about **designing circuits** that carefully manipulate qubits to reach useful results.


## 3. Why Quantum Matters for Business

Quantum computing is not built for browsing the internet or writing essays.

It is designed for problems like:

* Financial portfolio optimization
* Risk modeling
* Logistics routing
* Drug discovery
* Large-scale simulation
* Cryptography and security

These problems are hard because they have **too many possible outcomes**.

Quantum computing is powerful because it explores huge problem spaces in parallel.
